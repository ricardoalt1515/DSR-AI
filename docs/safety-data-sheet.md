### Precautionary Statements

#### Prevention

- Obtain special instructions before use
- Do not handle until all safety precautions have been read and understood
- Keep away from heat/sparks/open flames/hot surfaces — No smoking
- Keep container tightly closed
- Ground/bond container and receiving equipment
- Use explosion-proof electrical/ventilating/lighting equipment
- Use only non-sparking tools
- Take precautionary measures against static discharge
- Wash hands thoroughly after handling
- Do not breathe dust/fume/gas/mist/vapours/spray
- Do not eat, drink or smoke when using this product
- Use only outdoors or in a well-ventilated area
- Contaminated work clothing should not be allowed out of the workplace
- Avoid release to the environment
- Wear protective gloves/protective clothing/eye protection/face protection
- In case of inadequate ventilation, wear respiratory protection

#### Response

- **IF ON SKIN:** Take off immediately all contaminated clothing. Rinse skin with water/shower
- **IF INHALED:** Remove person to fresh air and keep comfortable for breathing
- **IF IN EYES:** Rinse cautiously with water for several minutes. Remove contact lenses if present and easy to do. Continue rinsing
- If exposed or concerned: Get medical advice/attention
- Get medical advice/attention if you feel unwell
- If skin irritation or rash occurs: Get medical advice/attention
- If eye irritation persists: Get medical advice/attention
- If experiencing respiratory symptoms: Call a POISON CENTER/doctor
- Take off contaminated clothing and wash it before reuse
- **In case of fire:** Use dry chemical, carbon dioxide, dry sand and foam compound to extinguish

#### Storage

- Keep container tightly closed
- Store in a well-ventilated place. Keep cool
- Store locked up
- Store in accordance with local/regional/national/international regulations

#### Disposal

- Dispose of contents/container in accordance with local/regional/national/international regulations

## 5. Fire-Fighting Measures

### Extinguishing Media
- Dry chemical
- Carbon dioxide
- Dry sand
- Foam compound

### Fire-Fighting Procedures
- Shut off flammable material to the fire and extinguish with appropriate agent
- Cool the surrounding area with water spray to avoid fire spread (straight stream water may cause fire spread)
- Move containers from fire area if possible
- If containers cannot be moved, cool surrounding area with water spray
- Fire-fighting should be done from upwind
- Wear proper protective equipment such as supplied-air mask

---

## 6. Accidental Release Measures

- Evacuate people downwind and prohibit entry to spill area
- Recovery work should be done from upwind with proper protective equipment (gas mask, etc.)
- Remove ignition sources immediately
- Strictly prohibit use of fire around spill area
- Use non-sparking equipment during recovery
- Prevent discharge to rivers or waterways

### Small Spills
- Absorb with inert materials (dry sand) or absorbent materials (oil mat)
- Recover into empty containers
- Wipe area with cloth

### Large Spills
- Dike with inert materials (earth, sand) to prevent drainage
- Transfer liquids and solids to empty containers for recovery or disposal

---

## 7. Handling and Storage

### Handling
- Wear proper protective equipment to avoid inhalation and contact with eyes, skin, and clothing
- Always handle from upwind
- Prevent leakage or spill; avoid generating steam unnecessarily
- Avoid rough handling (tipping, dropping, impacting containers)
- Wash emptied containers and store in designated area
- Eliminate ignition sources (sparks, flames) in work area
- Avoid contact with strong oxidizing agents
- Use explosion-proof electrical equipment with proper grounding
- Keep containers sealed or use local exhaust ventilation
- In case of inadequate ventilation, wear respiratory protection
- Ground/bond container and receiving equipment
- Use only non-sparking tools
- Take precautionary measures against static discharge
- Do not eat, drink or smoke when using this product
- Use only outdoors or in well-ventilated areas
- Contaminated work clothing should not be allowed out of workplace

### Storage
- Avoid direct sunlight
- Keep containers tightly closed; avoid contact with air
- Use explosion-proof electrical equipment with proper grounding
- Do not store with strong oxidizers
- Store in a well-ventilated place; keep cool
- Store locked up
- Store in accordance with local/regional/national/international regulations

## 11. Toxicological Information

### Acute Toxicity

#### Cyclohexane
| Route | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Oral (Rat) | LD₅₀ = 12,705 mg/kg |
| Dermal (Rabbit) | LD = 180,000 mg/kg |
| Inhalation (Mouse) | LC₅₀ = 70,000 mg/m³/2H |

#### 2-Ethylhexanoic Acid
| Route | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Oral (Rat) | LD₅₀ = 3,000 mg/kg |
| Dermal (Rabbit) | LD₅₀ = 1,140 mg/kg |

### Skin Corrosion/Irritation
- Cyclohexane: Rabbit 1548 mg/2D
- 2-Ethylhexanoic acid: Rabbit 450 mg (Mild)
- May cause irritation and sensitization to skin

### Eye Damage/Irritation
- 2-Ethylhexanoic acid: Rabbit 20 mg (Severe)
- May cause conjunctivitis with eye contact

### Mutagenicity
- Cyclohexane: E.coli DNA adduct 10 μmol/L

### Carcinogenicity
- **IARC Category 1** (Nickel compounds): The agent is carcinogenic to humans

### Reproductive/Developmental Toxicity
- Cyclohexane: Rat Inhalation NOAEL = 2,500 ppm
- 2-Ethylhexanoic acid: Rat Oral TDLo = 1,803 mg/kg

### Other Hazards
- Risk of effects from single and repeated exposure
